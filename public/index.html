Therapeutic Massage

What is Numinous Bodywork?

Numinous Bodywork is an integrative method seeking to be in touch with the wonder of inherent health, aligning experience with sensation, while assessing subtle movements within living tissue.

When working with someone, the uniqueness of the individual and their relationship to their body in present moment is the foundation of this work. Considering the interplay of physical, emotional, and spiritual experience lived everyday, our individual body presents as various shapes throughout one day. Some of those shapes take on memory and history providing a common form from which each of us derives our living and moving experience.

Pain and discomfort may arrive when our common, learned body form presents differently today; such as a recent accident, injury, or even just sleeping in a funny bed. Pain and discomfort may also show up when our body moves and breathes at a distance from our inherent source of health and optimal alignment of our structure, which may present as chronic pain and movement dysfunction.

Ultimately, Numinous Bodywork attempts to bring your somatic awareness in touch with your movement potential. Walking pain free and with ease, we have the ability to truly relax, opening doors to healthy possibility. When we are fully embodied, we exist in touch with wonder.

What happens during a session?

Numinous Bodywork integrates various approaches and concepts about the living body to access the uniqueness that is you. To begin, we briefly check in around your lived experience and recent movement history. From there, we assess the relationship between your bones and gravity. We agree on a plan for the session incorporating your goals and intention for the work.

At the heart of Numinous Bodywork, we listen through touch to the fluid dynamics of the system. These dynamics include circulation, breath, neurofascial signaling, visceral movement and the inflammatory response. Following the subtle movement of these dynamics inform the direction of the work. Throughout a session, there will be contact to fascia at deep and superficial layers.

Seeking optimal alignment provides our body with access to the inherent wisdom of our bones, our structure, and our movement.
